About Me

I hold an M.Sc. in Biomedical Engineering from BUET, where my thesis explored tannic acid crosslinked gelatin-CMC electrospun matrices for combating antibacterial resistance in infected wounds. This work involved developing dual-functional biomaterials that promote wound healing while managing infections through in vitro, in vivo, and in silico evaluations. As a Senior Product Development Engineer at BioEnclave—a company dedicated to regenerative healthcare solutions—I optimize advanced wound dressings like CuraGel™ and AmnioVive™, coordinating clinical trials and collaborating on innovative medical devices. With hands-on experience in electrospinning, nanoparticle synthesis, and molecular simulations, I'm eager to pursue a Ph.D. to further advance drug delivery and tissue engineering in resource-constrained settings.
